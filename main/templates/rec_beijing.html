How to choose?

Price is going to be the deciding factor for most people. While often prices are relatively similar across travel sites, it’s not entirely unusual to see a huge discount on one site as they’ve done a special limited-time deal directly with the hotel. Certain travel sites have better prices in certain regions, but there’s no site which can deliver the cheapest price 100% of the time so it pays to look around.


Whether or not a travel site has good availability in the region you’re looking to travel to is also very important. You can often find that some sites have more than double the amount of hotels available in a particular city when compared with others. But the number of hotels isn’t everything, whether or not they actually have available rooms to book is very important. Sometimes travel sites will “bulk purchase” rooms at a hotel, which might mean that they still have availability after other sites have sold out.


Check for any hidden fees like booking fees when you’re booking. Luckily, none of the major players are charging booking fees these days however some of the smaller sites do. Make you you’re compare apples with apples when you’re looking at the final price. Also, which payment methods the site accepts might be important. While everyone accepts MasterCard and Visa, if you’re looking to pay with PayPal, Google Pay or Apple Pay your options might be more limited. Check our reviews to get a comprehensive list of who accepts which payment methods.


The cancellation policy is also something you need to consider. While a lot of rooms are offered with free cancellation these days, don’t take it as a given. Different sites might list the same room with different policies, while other sites will offer a room with a choice of policies, so make sure you check before you click!


Finally, reputation is of course very important. Some sites just haven a much better reputation when it comes to looking after their customers, although they’re all far from perfect. Lots of online review sites like Trustpilot offer reviews of these sites however they’re to be taken with a grain of salt. Some travel sites actively try to encourage good reviews from their customer whereas others don’t pay too much attention to these sites and instead let their product do the talking.
